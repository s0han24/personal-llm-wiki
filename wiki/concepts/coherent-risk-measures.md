# Coherent Risk Measures
Coherent Risk Measures are a class of mathematical functions evaluating the risk of random outcomes that satisfy specific axioms identified by Artzner et al. (translation invariance, subadditivity, positive homogeneity, and monotonicity). [[CVaR]] is the most prominent coherent risk measure, unlike [[VaR]].
